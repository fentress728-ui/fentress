Original Lodge

The Jamestown Masonic Hall

Jamestown Lodge No. 281 was chartered by the Grand Lodge of Tennessee on October 6, 1859. The Jamestown Masonic Hall was completed in 1893. It was a statuesque building and one newspaper was quoted as saying the Hall was a sign of progress for the “sleepy town of Jamestown”. The lower story was a church with each denomination taking its turn for services: the Christian Church, the Methodist, and the Baptists. Reverend A.B. Wright, the early Fentress Countian who rode the circuits as a Methodist preacher, wrote in his journals that he preached in the courthouse in Jamestown before the Masonic building was built. He also preached in the homes in surrounding communities. He died before the Masonic Hall was built.

This building served at different periods as a church, a school, and as a polling place for elections. The Methodist Church met in this building while the present Methodist building was constructed and completed in 1951.

This Masonic Hall, with its dreams as a landmark of grandeur for the future, was in a state of disrepair in the 1950’s when it was sold and torn down—making way for the shopping center just below the location of the grand old building, which stood on the knoll among the pine trees North of Wheeler Avenue.
